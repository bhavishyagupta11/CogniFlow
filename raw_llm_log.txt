RAW:\nBERT, which stands for Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers, is a language representation model designed to pre-train deep bidirectional representations from unlabeled text [1]. Unlike other recent models, BERT conditions on both left and right context across all layers [1].

The architecture of the BERT model is a multi-layer bidirectional Transformer encoder [2]. Two sizes have been released: BERT-base, with 12 layers, 768 hidden units, 12 attention heads, and 110 million parameters; and BERT-large, with 24 layers, 1024 hidden units, 16 attention heads, and 340 million parameters [2]. Input representations for BERT are formed by summing token, segmentation, and position embeddings, utilizing WordPiece embeddings with a 30,000-token vocabulary [2, 3].

BERT is pre-trained using tasks such as a binarized next sentence prediction task [2]. Fine-tuning BERT for various downstream tasks is straightforward due to its self-attention mechanism, which allows for minimal changes; task-specific inputs and outputs are plugged into BERT, and all parameters are fine-tuned end-to-end [3]. BERT has achieved state-of-the-art results on eleven NLP tasks, including GLUE, SQuAD v1.1, and SQuAD v2.0 [3]. Additionally, BERT models can be used in other architectures, such as the Dense Passage Retriever (DPR) component of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) models, where two BERT models encode queries and documents into dense vectors for retrieval [4].\n---END---\nRAW:\nBERT, which stands for Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers, is a language representation model designed to pre-train deep bidirectional representations from unlabeled text [1].
